Autonomous AI agents

AI is more than just chatbots! AI agents can independently handle obstacles, make decisions, and adapt
-just like humans do.

On this website, I’m introducing `Turing Tactics`, named after Alan Turing and his famous test. The slogan is: `Autonomous AI agents for dynamic environments`.

But what does that mean exactly? Think of:

  • Robotics: A robot vacuum that comes up with a new route when you threw your sports bag in the corner again;
  • Recommendation: A webshop that gives real-time, personalized product recommendations based on your shopping cart;
  • Optimisation: A smart house agent who controls appliances to reduce your energy bill.
  • Gaming agents: A video game with smart teammates, opponents, and NPCs that adapt to your playing style or level.

    • In all of these examples, the AI agent operates independently in a dynamically changing environment. This is made possible by reinforcement learning, which is learning through rewards and penalties -just like we do as humans!

    I’ve been fascinated by this technology for years. With a background in psychology and neuroscience, I started by studying human behavior and decision-making. Later, I shifted into a technical career, working as a data and cloud engineer, and AI scientist. I love that in Turing Tactics, everything comes together.

    One thing I’ve noticed: Big Tech is leading the way in applying this technology. All social media algorithms, and the latest version of ChatGPT, rely heavily on reinforcement learning. But there’s huge untapped potential for smaller companies to benefit as well, from efficiency gains and increased value to better user experiences.

    Turing Tactics offers:

  • Brainstorming sessions and strategic advice
  • Custom AI agents, with low-barrier proof of concept projects that can scale based on results
  • Workshops for technical teams to learn how to design, develop, improve and maintain AI agents themselves
